10 Tips for Safe Social Networking

by GOsafeonline | 22 January 2015

Social networking sites such as Facebook, LinkedIn and Twitter allow users to connect with others to share information such as photos, videos and personal messages. However, as the popularity of these social networking sites grows, so does the risk in using them.

You may be thinking what kind of risk one may face when using these social networking sites. What you may not know is that there could be links in your newsfeed or messages that directs you to a malicious website, or there could be someone gathering information about you in order to steal your identity. Cyber criminals could also use your data to craft a targeted email in order to trick you into giving more personal information.

Cyber criminals are enjoying greater degrees of success on social networking sites due to the fact that users are more likely to click on links that are sent by their supposedly ‘friends’.

So how do you protect yourself on social networking sites? Below are ten tips on how to do so.

10 Tips for Safe Social Networking

  1. Be cautious about how much personal information you provide on social networking sites.

    A common way for cybercriminals to break into your account is by clicking the “Forgot your password?” link. The answer to your security question could be found in your profile or it could be found in one of your post. The more information you provide, the easier it is for hacker to steal your identity.

    If the social networking site allows you to create your own security question, ensure that the answer isn’t one that can be determined from a quick search on the internet.

  2. Use caution when you click on links

    Even if these links are in a message from your friend, do exercise caution when clicking on the link. It could be the case where your friend’s account has been hijacked and it is sending malicious links to everyone on their contact list.

    Treat links in message on these sites as you would in email.

  3. Be selective on who you accept as a friend on your social network

    Identity thieves might create fake profiles in order to get information from you. People are not always who they say they are.

  4. Type the address of your social networking sites directly into your browser

    If you click a link that leads you to a social networking website through an e-mail or another website, it may actually be a phishing website that is design to look like the legitimate website in order to trick users into providing their user name and password.

    This doesn’t apply only to social networking sites but any links you receive via email or any other website.

  5. Be careful when you install third party applications on your social networking sites

    Many social networking sites allow you to download third-party applications that let you do more with your personal page. However, cyber criminals can make use of these applications to steal your personal information without your knowledge.

    Exercise the same precaution that you were to take when you download any program from the web.

  6. Whatever you post online is permanent

    Assume that whatever information you posted on your social networking site is permanent. Think twice before you post anything online.

  7. Customize privacy settings  

    Use privacy settings to control who can see various aspects of your personal information.

    Learn how sites can use your information by reviewing the site’s privacy policy.

  8. Never use the same password for all your account

    This applies not only to social networking sites but to all your online accounts. This ensures that a breach in one of your account doesn’t put your other accounts at risk.

  9. Do not allow social networking sites to scan your email address book.

    When you first create your account, you might receive a prompt asking whether you would like to “Find your friends”. By providing your email address, the site will scan your address book in order to find out if your contacts are on the network. However, the site might use this information to send email messages to everyone on your contact list. 

  10. Think twice before you use social networking sites at work

    Accessing social networking sites in the office would pose a security risk as it open the company’s computer system to virus attacks. For example, this can happen by opening a malicious email attachment or by clicking on a link that downloads malicious programs onto your computer.

    Be careful with social networking sites.
